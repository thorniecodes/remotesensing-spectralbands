# Remote Sensing Band Guide

Remote sensing satellites capture reflected and emitted energy across distinct portions of the electromagnetic spectrum. Each **spectral band** is tuned to a specific wavelength range, making it sensitive to different surface properties — from vegetation health and water clarity to built-up land and geological formations.

This table covers the major bands used in Earth observation: the wavelengths they occupy, the sensors that collect them, the spectral indices they power, and their primary applications.
